names _____________________________________________________________________________________________
-- particle standards
   -- Currently "PDG" and "alternate"
   -- The name "alternate" says nothing about what it is, and makes it unclear what to do if we add
      another format (e.g., MCNP has a muddled mixture between PDG and alternate, so that could
      potentially define a third format, and LHCb has proposed a format that extends PDG)
   -- The "alternate" format is used in, for example, Claude Amsler's 2015 "Nuclear and Particle
      Physics".  Ironically, Claude Amsler is also the lead author on the official document
      outlining the PDG format.
   -- The difference is mostly related to the sign of antiparticles: PDG uses the sign of the
      particle in question, while the "alternate" format says that antiparticles take the sign of
      their corresponding non-anti particle.  For example, the antiparticle of the positive sigma
      baryon would be either the negative sigma antibaryon (PDG) or the positive sigma antibaryon
      (alternate), and the symbol would be either Σ- or Σ+ (with the appropriate overbar).
